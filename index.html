How do we build cardiac digital twins personalized enough to represent an individual patient's physiology, and reliable enough to be used as tools in precision medicine? That question drives my work modeling patient-specific atrial electrophysiology and arrhythmia risk.

¿Cómo construir gemelos digitales cardíacos que sean suficientemente personalizados para representar la fisiología de un paciente y puedan utilizarse como herramientas confiables para la medicina de precisión? About me Sobre mí

I am a researcher building patient-specific cardiac digital twins at the Institute of Biomedical Engineering, Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), focused on atrial electrophysiology and arrhythmia risk. My work integrates clinical measurements — electroanatomical mapping, refractory periods, fibrosis — into computational models to make simulations reliable enough for real clinical decisions.

Jugar

Click or drag on the tissue to place ablation lesions (black) and block the rotors sustaining the arrhythmia. Watch the colors: bright turquoise is a fresh wavefront, fading to lilac as it becomes refractory, dark purple is resting tissue. Careless lesions can wall off a small island of tissue and trigger a brand-new little rotor inside it — that's not a bug, it mirrors a real clinical risk of ablation lines with gaps. Try to reach 100% resting tissue with as few lesions as possible.

Haz clic o arrastra sobre el tejido para colocar lesiones de ablación (negro) y bloquear los rotores que sostienen la arritmia. Observa los colores: turquesa brillante es un frente de onda recién activado, que se va a lila al volverse refractario, y el morado oscuro es tejido en reposo. Lesiones mal puestas pueden aislar una pequeña isla de tejido y generar un nuevo mini-rotor ahí dentro — no es un error, refleja un riesgo clínico real de las líneas de ablación con huecos. Intenta llegar al 100% de tejido en reposo usando la menor cantidad de lesiones posible.
